


This video explores what it really means to be "fluent" with AI and why this matters. We discuss how AI Fluency involves developing practical skills, knowledge, insights, and values that help you interact with AI systems in ways that are effective, efficient, ethical, and safe. We also introduce three ways people engage with AI:

- Automation: The AI completes specific tasks based on your instructions.
- Augmentation: You and AI collaborate as creative thinking and task execution partners.
- Agency: You configure AI to work independently on your behalf, establishing its knowledge and behavior patterns rather than just giving it specific tasks.

## Video summary

This part explains **what AI fluency means and why it is important**. As AI becomes more integrated into daily life—helping with writing, research, brainstorming, and decision-making—simply having access to AI does not mean people know **how to use it effectively or responsibly**.

**AI fluency** is the ability to interact with AI in ways that are **effective, efficient, ethical, and safe**. It is not about memorizing prompts or technical tricks, but about developing adaptable skills, knowledge, and judgment that remain useful as AI technology evolves.

The lecture describes **three main ways people interact with AI**:

1. **Automation** – AI performs a specific task based on clear instructions (e.g., summarizing a document or drafting an email).
    
2. **Augmentation** – Humans and AI collaborate to think, explore ideas, and solve problems together (e.g., brainstorming story ideas or working through a design problem).
    
3. **Agency** – AI operates more independently, acting on behalf of the user based on defined goals or behavior patterns (e.g., automatically sorting emails or powering interactive systems).
    

Each mode has different strengths, and many projects may use **all three approaches together**. While automation is the most common starting point, **augmentation and agency often unlock the most creative and powerful uses of AI**.

The key idea is that AI should not only be seen as a **tool**, but also as a **collaborator or co-creator**. Because of this shift, humans must develop new skills, which are organized in the **AI Fluency Framework**, centered around four key competencies that the rest of the course will explore.
