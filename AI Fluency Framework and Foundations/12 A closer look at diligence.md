## Video: A closer look at diligence

**Video Summary: The Diligence Competency (AI Fluency series)**

This video covers the **diligence** competency — the ethical and safety-focused pillar of AI fluency. While delegation, description (prompting), and other skills focus on **effectiveness & efficiency**, diligence is about **responsibility, ethics, and safety**.

### Core idea of Diligence

Diligence means **taking full personal responsibility** for your AI interactions. It’s like driving a car: you don’t just focus on getting there fast — you also drive safely, follow rules, and consider how your actions affect others.

Diligence answers bigger questions such as:

- What are the implications of using this AI?
- Who might be affected by the output or any errors?
- Who owns or can access the data I’m sharing?
- Does this align with my values, my organization’s policies, and ethical standards?

### The 3 Pillars of Diligence

1. **Creation Diligence** Being critical and intentional about **which AI systems** you choose and **how** you use them. Key questions to ask **before** using any AI:
    
    - How was this model trained? What data was used?
    - Who owns the data I’m inputting right now?
    - Who might have access to it?
    - Does this service protect privacy and security?
    - Is this allowed by my company/academic rules? Example: Never paste sensitive company data into an AI unless you’ve checked the privacy policy and your organization permits it.
2. **Transparency Diligence** Being open and honest about AI’s role in your work. Ask yourself:
    
    - Who needs to know that AI helped create this?
    - How and when should I disclose it?
    - What level of detail is appropriate?
    
    Example: If you used AI to draft a team proposal, tell your colleagues which parts were AI-assisted so everyone can collaborate honestly and maintain trust.
    
3. **Deployment Diligence** Taking full ownership of the final output — **you** (not the AI) are responsible for accuracy, appropriateness, and consequences. This means:
    
    - Verify every fact
    - Check for biases or errors
    - Ensure proper usage rights and sources
    - Meet the same professional standards you would without AI
    
    Example: A journalist using AI to help write an article must still fact-check everything to the same standard as if they wrote it themselves.
    

### Practical Advice

- Develop your own personal AI guidelines that match your ethics and values.
- In professional settings, learn your organization’s policies and industry standards.
- Stay informed — AI laws and regulations are still evolving.

### Final Recap

The diligence competency has three parts that work together: **Creation Diligence** (choose systems thoughtfully) **Transparency Diligence** (be honest about AI’s role) **Deployment Diligence** (own and verify the final output)

Diligence ensures your AI use is not only **effective and efficient**, but also **ethical, safe, and accountable**. It reminds us that we all play a role in making AI fair and beneficial to society through our own responsible behavior.

#### **Step 1: Understand Diligence Statements**

_Estimated time: 3 minutes_

A diligence statement is a transparent acknowledgment of AI's role in your work, along with your commitment to responsibility for the final output. Here's an example:

"In creating this [document/project/content], I collaborated with [AI assistant name] to assist with [specific tasks: drafting, research, editing, etc.]. I affirm that all AI-generated and co-created content underwent thorough review and evaluation. The final output accurately reflects my understanding, expertise, and intended meaning. While AI assistance was instrumental in the process, I maintain full responsibility for the content, its accuracy, and its presentation. This disclosure is made in the spirit of transparency and to acknowledge the role of AI in the creation process."

#### Step 2: Reflect on Your AI Collaboration

_Estimated time: 5 minutes_

Think about your work on the course project and consider:

- Creation Diligence:
    - Which AI systems did you choose to work with and why?
    - What data or information did you share with the AI?
    - Were there any privacy, security, or ethical considerations in your choices?
- Transparency Diligence:
    - Who is the audience for your project output?
    - What expectations might they have regarding AI disclosure?
    - How specifically did AI contribute to different aspects of your work?
- Deployment Diligence:
    - What steps did you take to verify the accuracy and appropriateness of AI contributions?
    - How did you ensure the final output meets your standards and requirements?
