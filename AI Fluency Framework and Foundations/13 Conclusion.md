### The 4 Core Competencies (The Four “D’s”)

|Competency|Focus|What it means (in simple terms)|
|---|---|---|
|**Delegation**|Effectiveness & Efficiency|Deciding what to do yourself, what to give to AI, and how to split the work (based on problem awareness + platform awareness + smart task division).|
|**Description**|Effectiveness & Efficiency|How you communicate with AI — clear prompts, context, examples, output format, steps, role/persona, etc. (also called product/process/performance description).|
|**Discernment**|Quality & Appropriateness|Critically evaluating AI outputs, reasoning process, and interaction (product/process/performance discernment). You must judge what’s good, accurate, or biased.|
|**Diligence**|Ethics, Safety & Responsibility|Taking ownership: choosing AI systems wisely (creation), being transparent about AI use (transparency), and standing behind the final result (deployment).|

### The 3 Ways We Work with AI

All four competencies apply across:

- **Automation** – AI does a specific task for you
- **Augmentation** – You and AI collaborate as thinking partners
- **Agency** – AI acts independently on your behalf

### Key Takeaways & Mindset for the Future

- Your **human expertise and judgment** are still the foundation — AI is a powerful partner, not a replacement.
- The best results come from **iterative collaboration** where humans and AI build on each other’s strengths.
- Clear communication (description) + critical evaluation (discernment) turns AI from a simple tool into a true thinking partner.
- You are **always accountable** — be transparent and responsible for everything you create with AI.
- These skills are not mastered in one course. They grow through **regular practice**.
- The framework is designed to stay useful even as AI keeps evolving.

### Professor’s Closing Message

AI is not a silver bullet or magic solution. It is only as useful, effective, ethical, and safe as **we** make it.

→ Invest in your own expertise. → Practice the art of thoughtful conversation with AI. → Take responsibility for what you create together.
