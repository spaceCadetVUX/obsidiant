## Video Summary: Crafting Effective Prompts (AI Fluency series)

This video teaches the practical skill of **prompting** — the real-world application of the **description competency** in AI fluency. Prompting = clearly communicating **what** you want, **how** you want it done, and **how** you want to interact with the AI.

It compares prompting to giving clear instructions to an eager but new colleague. The video uses **Claude** as the example, but the principles apply to almost any AI (ChatGPT, Grok, Gemini, etc.).

### 6 Foundational Prompting Tips

1. **Give Claude (or any AI) rich context** Be specific about **what**, **why**, and **who you are**. Bad: “Tell me about climate change.” Good: “Explain three major impacts of climate change on agriculture in tropical regions with examples from the past decade. I’m preparing for a job interview at an agricultural research lab in Indonesia. I have a degree in ecology but no specific knowledge on climate change. Write a summary that helps me speak intelligently in the interview.”
2. **Show examples of what good looks like** (Few-shot / n-shot prompting) Instead of describing the style, just show it. Example: Give 2–3 pairs of “Technical → Plain language” before asking it to convert a whole manual.
3. **Specify output constraints** (format, length, style, colors, etc.) Tell the AI exactly how you want the answer delivered. Example: “Create a clean, modern, single-page art portfolio website with these sections… Use a sunset color palette… Add dark/light mode toggle… Make navigation sticky and responsive.”
4. **Break complex tasks into steps** (Chain-of-Thought prompting) List the exact process you want the AI to follow. Especially useful when the task has many possible approaches or relies on your domain expertise.
5. **Ask the AI to think first** (before giving the final answer) Prompt: “Before answering, please think through this problem carefully. Consider different factors, potential constraints, and various approaches before recommending the best solution.” This improves quality and lets you see the AI’s reasoning (very useful for catching mistakes).
6. **Define Claude’s role, style, or tone** (persona prompting) Tell the AI who it should “be.” Examples:
    - “Explain how rainbows form from the perspective of an experienced science teacher speaking to a bright 10-year-old.”
    - “As a UX design expert, review this wireframe and suggest three improvements focused on navigation and accessibility.”

### Bonus “Secret Weapon”

When you’re stuck: Ask the AI to **improve your prompt** for you. “I’m trying to achieve [goal]. I’m not sure how to phrase it for the best results. Can you help me craft an effective prompt?”

### How to iterate & improve

- Your first prompt rarely works perfectly — that’s normal.
- Refine by adding more context, examples, steps, or constraints.
- Ask for 3 variations.
- Request different formats (bullet points, table, interactive artifact, etc.).
- Ask “How confident are you?” on factual answers.
- Start a fresh conversation if it goes off track.

### Common mistakes to avoid

- Assuming the AI can read your mind
- Being vague about what success looks like
- Dumping multiple unrelated tasks into one prompt
- Not giving feedback or iterating

### Final takeaway

Effective prompting is **iterative experimentation** that blends timeless human communication skills with a few AI-specific tricks. The 6 principles + the habit of asking the AI to help you write better prompts will dramatically improve your results with any AI system.

Practice these and you’ll go from “okay” results to consistently excellent, tailored, high-quality outputs.
## Effective Prompting Techniques

This video explores practical skills for crafting effective prompts when working with AI assistants like Claude. We explain that prompt engineering is simply the practice of designing effective instructions for AI systems, combining familiar human communication principles with AI-specific considerations. We introduce six foundational techniques: giving context, showing examples of desired outputs, specifying constraints, breaking complex tasks into steps, asking the AI to think first, and defining the AI's role or tone. We also share troubleshooting strategies for when responses aren't quite right and highlight common patterns that lead to successful interactions.

#### Key takeaways

- Effective prompting combines clear communication principles with AI-specific techniques
- Six foundational prompting techniques:
    - Give context: Be specific about what you want, why you want it, and relevant background
    - Show examples: Demonstrate the output style or format you're looking for
    - Specify constraints: Clearly define format, length, and other output requirements
    - Break complex tasks into steps: Guide the AI through multi-step reasoning
    - Ask the AI to think first: Give space for the AI to work through its process
    - Define the AI's role or tone: Specify how you want the AI to communicate
- The "secret weapon": Ask the AI itself to help improve your prompt
- Successful prompting is iterative (and perhaps also collaborative with the AI!). Expect to refine your approach based on results
- Common successful patterns include providing clear task overviews, format specifications, explicit constraints, and relevant background information
